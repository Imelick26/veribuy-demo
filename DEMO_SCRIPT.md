## PRE-INSPECTION INTELLIGENCE

This is where VeriBuy starts earning its keep — before your buyer even touches the vehicle.

Based on the VIN, VeriBuy pulls known failure points specific to this exact year, model, and engine — the 2024 Bronco Sport 1.5-liter EcoBoost. This isn't generic data. It's sourced from NHTSA complaints, Technical Service Bulletins, and real owner reports.

Look at what comes back.

Head Gasket Failure — flagged Critical. This is a known pattern across 2021 to 2024 EcoBoost engines. Cylinder two-three breach under load. Estimated repair: $2,800 to $4,200.

Fuel Injector Leak — also Critical. Micro-cracks at the O-ring seat, and multiple NHTSA complaints cite fire risk. Repair: $600 to $1,100.

Water Pump Premature Failure — High severity. Internal bearing fails between 18,000 and 35,000 miles. This is the most-reported issue on NHTSA for this model year. Repair: $900 to $1,400.

12-Volt Battery and BCM Defect — High. The Body Control Module has widespread reports of parasitic drain, killing the battery in two to three days. Repair can run up to $1,800.

There are also Moderate concerns around the rear drive unit and windshield stress cracking.

But here's what matters — VeriBuy doesn't just list risks. It gives your buyer a specific inspection checklist for each one. Smell for raw fuel at cold start. Look for dried coolant below the water pump weep hole. Grab the pulley and check for bearing play. Your buyer knows exactly what to look for, even if they've never inspected this model before.

The 3D model shows where each risk area is located on the vehicle. VeriBuy turns your average buyer into a specialist on every vehicle they evaluate.

## GUIDED MEDIA CAPTURE

Now your buyer performs the physical inspection, and VeriBuy guides every step.

The platform specifies exactly which photos are needed — front centered, driver side, rear, engine bay, under-hood label — eight shots total, each with framing guidelines. Consistent framing enables VeriBuy's AI to perform damage detection and comparable matching across vehicles.

Every photo is automatically timestamped and GPS-tagged — a verifiable chain of evidence for the condition at the moment of inspection.

## INSPECTION FINDINGS

This is the moment of truth. VeriBuy analyzes the inspection data and delivers verified findings.

Condition Score: 64 out of 100. Broken down — Structural and Drivetrain scores a 42, that's a red flag. Cosmetic and Interior is at 78. Electronics and Software sits at 65.

Here's what was found.

Head Gasket Compromised — Critical. White residue on the oil cap. Coolant reservoir 40 percent low. Combustion gas detected in the coolant via block test. Repair: $2,800 to $4,200. This is structural — it affects both resale and long-term reliability.

Fuel Injector Leak Confirmed — High. VeriBuy flagged this as a known risk before the inspection even started. Now it's confirmed — wet spot on injector number three, raw fuel smell at cold start, and Recall 24V-123 has not been performed. Repair: $600 to $1,100. This is a safety recall that must be completed.

Driver Seat Tear on the Leather Bolster — Moderate. A 1.5-inch tear from normal entry and exit wear. Cosmetic, but it affects retail presentation. Repair: $200 to $350.

Every finding is backed by timestamped photo evidence and GPS coordinates. This isn't opinion — it's data.

In this case, the history shows a clean title, zero prior accidents, and four service records — but two open recalls that align directly with the fuel injector and BCM risks VeriBuy already identified. The history corroborates the physical inspection, which moves the overall confidence level to High.

## MARKET ANALYSIS & PRICING

Now VeriBuy answers the most important question — what should you actually pay for this vehicle?

Live comparable listings show AutoTrader at an average of $23,495 across 28 listings in Portland. Cars.com at $24,200 across 34 listings in Eugene. Wholesale at $20,100 across 12 listings in the Pacific Northwest.

But those are prices for clean vehicles. This one isn't clean. So VeriBuy builds a condition-adjusted price.

Start with the wholesale baseline for a clean unit at 21,000 miles — $20,100. Subtract $3,500 for the head gasket. Subtract $850 for the fuel injector. Subtract $275 for the seat bolster. Subtract $200 for the overall condition score adjustment.

Recommended Maximum Acquisition Price: $15,275.

VeriBuy gives you buy zones. Strong Buy at or below $14,000. Fair Buy from $14,000 to $15,300. Anything above $15,300 and you're overpaying.

A vehicle that looks clean at 21,000 miles — the kind that could easily trade at $20,000 wholesale — actually needs to be purchased under $15,300 to make economic sense. That's a nearly $5,000 gap that VeriBuy just protected you from.
